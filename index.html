PROSPECTS OF HORTCULTURE IN INDIA

Horticulture is made up of two words Hortus + Cultura, which means garden agriculture.

    The ambit of agriculture is big.

  • Promiculture (Fruits)
  • Olericulture (Vegetables)
  • Ornamental (Flower)
  • Landscaping
  • Arboriculture (Trees)
India is 2nd largest horticulture producing country. Many of the products are top produce in the world.
There are many cause and factors for such growth and market.

1- Cheap and ample manpower:
The vegetable and fruit require a lot of manpower to carefully plant and manage the produce.

Various Govt schemes supplementing horticulture
National horticulture mission (NHM) under mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture has propelled the growthof horticultureproduce like anything
Continuous growth of area vide Horticulture.
Horticultural foodgrain production (for 2018-2019 data)

High yield:area ratio
High yield to area ratio for the horticulture produces attracts many farmers

High export value: Horticulture product are of high cultural value exported to countries like UAE, Bangladesh etc.

Multipurpose usage
Apart from local dietory usage the horticulture produce is used in many places like

  • Food processing
  • Perfume and cosmetics
  • Pharma companies

Problems of horticulture

  • Lack of Storage
    Horticulture produce are perishable and readily rotten hence proper storage with cold storage facility is necessary. Timely transportation of the produce from cold storage to villages and market .

  • Variety of horticulture produce
    The variety and popularity is not as much as food grains.

  • Incessant use of fertilizers and pesticides
    Natural and ZB farming still has few takers in Indian farming community

Still horticulture has to get traction in India, as much as foodgrains, specially when India has challenges against malnutrition. Mission like "Mission Postian by Go I" should also integtate sensitizing of farmers as well as consumers to balance both demand and supply.
